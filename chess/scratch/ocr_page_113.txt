We have already seen that White is very fond of the move h2-h3. In
this particular instance it may not be the most accurate as it allows
Black to carry out his queenside advance. Let's have a look at a couple
of important alternatives.
1) 9 Rel is well met by 9..exd4 10 Nxd4 Ngd!. The first point is that if
White takes on g4 Black takes on d4 and this simplification is in
Black's favour. Secondly, if White kicks the knight with 11 h3 Black
replies 11...Qf6! counter-attacking against the knight on d4
2) 9 Qe2 is played quite often as now 9...b5?! 10 cxb5 axb5 11 Rd1! is
quite promising for White. After 9 Qc2 Black usually reverts to the
Gallagher System as White will no longer be able to play the move
Qxd6. For example, 9...exd4 10 Nxd4 Re8 11 Rd1 RbS followed by the
usual advance on the queenside.
